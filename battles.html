FAMOUS BATTLES

1947

Viet Bac Thu Dong Campaign

The Việt Bắc Thu Đông Campaign of 1947 saw French forces attacking the Việt Bắc base to destroy the Việt Minh. Using guerrilla tactics and the mountainous terrain, the Việt Minh dispersed and wore down the enemy. They successfully defended the base, inflicted heavy losses on the French, and forced them to retreat.

1950

Bien Gioi Thu Dong Campaign

The Việt Bắc Thu Đông Campaign of 1947 saw French forces attacking the Việt Bắc base. The Việt Minh used guerrilla tactics and the terrain to wear down the enemy, ultimately defending the base and forcing the French to retreat.

1954

Dien Bien Phu Campaign

The Điện Biên Phủ Campaign (March-May 1954) was the decisive battle against French colonialism. Under General Võ Nguyên Giáp, the Việt Minh used siege and tunneling tactics to defeat the French. This victory led to the Geneva Accords, ending French presence in Indochina and advancing Vietnam’s independence struggle.

1968

Mau Than Campaign (30/1 - 28/3/1968)

The Tet Mau Than Campaign (1968) was a major military and popular uprising by the National Liberation Front of South Vietnam during the Tết Lunar New Year. It targeted key US military and Republic of Vietnam locations in most southern urban areas, playing a pivotal and consequential role.

1969

Tran Doi Thit Bam (10/5 - 20/5/1969)

The Battle of A Bia (May 10-20, 1969) in Thừa Thiên-Huế saw the US Army, with 2,000 soldiers and heavy artillery and air support, attack a hill held by the National Liberation Front of South Vietnam. Despite capturing the hill after 10 days of fierce fighting, the US forces withdrew a month later, leaving many casualties.

1975

Ho Chi Minh Campaign (30/4/1975)

The historic Ho Chi Minh Campaign in the Spring Offensive of 1975 marked the end of the resistance war against the US and the complete liberation of South Vietnam, unifying the country. It fulfilled President Hồ Chí Minh’s directive to “drive the Americans out, overthrow the puppet regime.”
