HO CHI MINH - THE GREAT LEADER

Ho Chi Minh, the founder and leader of the Communist Party of Vietnam, played a crucial role in leading the national independence movement. He is a national liberation hero and a world cultural figure.

"Nothing is more precious than independence and freedom."

VO NGUYEN GIAP - THE LEGENDARY GENERAL

Vo Nguyen Giap, one of the greatest generals of the 20th century, is renowned for his victory at Dien Bien Phu.

“The word ‘fear’ does not exist in our military thinking, because for us, nothing is more precious than independence and freedom. We are determined to fight the Americans and we always believe we will win.”

PRIME MINISTER PHAM VAN DONG - THE SKILLED DIPLOMAT

The first Prime Minister of Vietnam made significant contributions to international negotiations, helping to strengthen Vietnam’s position on the global stage.

“Diplomacy is the art of the possible.”

VICE PRESIDENT NGUYEN THI BINH - THE QUEEN OF VIET CONG

Nguyen Thi Binh - an outstanding revolutionary and diplomat of Vietnam. She played a crucial role at the Paris Conference, contributing to the signing of the Paris Agreement in 1973, which ended the Vietnam War. She continued to serve as Minister of Education and Vice President of Vietnam.

“The Vietnamese nation is one; Vietnamese people in the North and the South have the duty to fight against invaders.”

VO THI SAU - HEROINE OF THE PEOPLE’S ARMED FORCES

Vo Thi Sau - a daughter of Dat Do, Ba Ria, was a heroine in the resistance against the French. Joining the revolution at the age of 14, she carried out many important missions, notably eliminating a notorious village chief and disrupting a French rally.

“My only regret is not having eliminated all the colonial invaders and their traitorous collaborators.”
